landscape About Us

Just two hours northwest of Toronto, Blue Mountain is a haven of relaxation and rejuvenation surrounded by spectacular natural beauty. No matter the weather, there are things to do and family fun to be had right outside your door.

We are all grateful to the Canadian First Nations, Matis, and Inuit peoples, who have been the land's stewards and storytellers for millennia. We have the privilege of sharing Blue Mountain's beauty with others and are accountable for preserving it for future generations.

snowboarding Activities

We've put together some sample itineraries that, whether you're a returning guest or a first-time visitor, may serve as inspiration for your next "must do" list at Blue. We can help you check off every box for any experience you're looking for.


Explore the mountain on foot

A hike up one of Blue Mountain's hiking routes is a great way to start the day. The Niagara Escarpment's stunning landscapes and natural wonders can be seen from a variety of challenging trails, which all reward hikers with spectacular views. Listen to the audio below to get a glimpse of what you are in for.

Hiking Information
  • Hiking is self-guided and proper footwear is recommended. Lightweight sandals are not advised for this activity.
  • Hiking trails are open during regular operating hours. Some of the trails are modifies with lights so that they can be used at night.


Monterra Golf is Still Green

A course that encourages everyone to perform above their own expectations starting at the tee. Monterra Golf offers spectacular rounds with its rolling bent grass courses, 86 bunkers, ravines, creeks, lakes, elevated tee shots, and breathtaking mountain backdrop. In order for you to concentrate on the big picture, we take care of the tiny aspects like GPS tracking on all carts.

If you are serious about golf, then this is a destination to consider for your next vacation. There are advantages to booking a group reservation, and it might be the one time you are ok with scoring high with your friends.

Blue Mountain Explore Park

Did you know Blue Mountain is nestled in the heart of Ontario's largest apple growing region and home of award winning apple pie trail? Here, dozens of apple kinds are cultivated, including the popular Gala, Honey Crisp, and Ambrosia varieties.

Here you will find apple inspired art, learn the history of apple growing. But everyone says that this area is all about outdoor adventures so while you are tasting your way through apple country you will also have the chance to enlighten the family with adventure they will remember for years to come.
